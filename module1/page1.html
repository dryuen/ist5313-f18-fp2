In order to pass the test, you need to play their game. For example, let say the STAAR test asked you "Give me an example of a math problem that equals 4", most of you would say "2+2". This is a well known fact, 2+2 = 4. But the STAAR test says no, we wanted 3+1. You gave the right answer, but not in the form they requested. This program was designed to teach strategies to help you understand how to play the game and pass the test. You need 43% correct to pass. You have 5 hours to take the EOC, within those 5 hours you must work on a two part test (reading and writing). Understanding across Genres usually makes up 8 out of the 52 multiple choice questions.
